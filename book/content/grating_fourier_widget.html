Grating (real space)
Fourier transform (intensity)
A pure grating is a single spatial frequency, so its transform is just two points (ideally sharp Dirac peaks, drawn with a small finite size only so they stay visible). Their distance from the centre is the frequency, their angle is the direction, and their brightness follows the amplitude. Changing the phase slides the stripes on the left but leaves the transform unchanged, because its intensity does not depend on phase.
